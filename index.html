Together with a group for friends from my secondary school's O'Level Computing class, we took part in the SCDF x IBM Lifesavers' Challenge: Call for Code 2020. We were given 4 problem statements and we settled on "Preventing The Spread" which mainly focused on forest fires and wildfires in Singapore with the rise in temperatures due to global warming.

Our solution was a drone based idea of drones patrolling areas prone to such fires and report them to the SCDF when they are detected by the drones. This would reduce response times as there would be no need to wait for a civilian to report the fire. My main contribution was the development of one of the python codes of which it tallied up the number of incidents at each location and calculated the risk factor of a fire occurring at each location based on past incidents. This is to allow for better use of patrol drones at each area, regulating the number of drones based on the loactions' risk factor.

Ransomware: The Dangers & How to Avoid Them

Speakers: Diana Kelley, Security Curve | Charlie Smith, Barracuda | Steve Cobb, One Source | Shawn Bowen, World Fuel Services

The focus of the webinar is on ransomware. I learnt that the timeline and process of a ransomware attack is more complex than just one single party executing the entire process of the attack. As it turns out, the initial access to the victim is usually brokered out to third parties, who would then sell this access to the actual attacker who would conduct the ransomware attack. This means that the period of time between initial access and the actual attack can sometimes be very small and other times very large, making the process of identifying and catching all the threat actors more complicated.

Industry Engagement 2

Screenshot of webinar 2: How to Achieve Zero Trust Data Protection in the Cloud

How to Achieve Zero Trust Data Protection in the Cloud Certificate

Previous Next
How to Achieve Zero Trust Data Protection in the Cloud

Speakers: Jason Clark, Dustin Wilcox

Atttending this webinar learning about zero trust, i learnt that to achieve Zero Trust Data there must be both strategy and tactics. The strategies being, Reduce the attack surface, Complicate unauthorised access, and Rapidly detect to stop threats. The strategies alone are no good without the tactics, Secure Access Service Edge (SASE) + Zero Trust for the technologies, processes, people and partners. I chose this webinar as I because I want to know how and what the industry is currently doing for data security in the cloud, being relevant to my course in cybersecurity. I did gain some insight into how they do try to achieve zero trust data protection, but they did mention how it is difficult to properly implement good solutions when security was an afterthought, only implementing the zero trust data protection after the fact, and how it is much simpler and better to implement a clean single solution from the start.

For a service learning project we taught a group of seniors from Yong En Care Centre a few features, both basic and more advanced ones like breakout rooms, of the video calling application Zoom.

Though a little slow inthe beginning, we quickly pick up pace after learning that our Yong En participants were more tech-savvy than we expected and we could go faster and use more technical lingo without having to worry about them not understanding or getting left behind. I learnt from this experience that not all eldery are slow to pick up new skills. Seeing how much of a life long learner each of our participants were, i too aspire to be a Life-Long Learner.
