Together with a group for friends from my secondary school's O'Level Computing class, we took part in the SCDF x IBM Lifesavers' Challenge: Call for Code 2020. We were given 4 problem statements and we settled on "Preventing The Spread" which mainly focused on forest fires and wildfires in Singapore with the rise in temperatures due to global warming. Our solution was a drone based idea of drones patrolling areas prone to such fires and report them to the SCDF when they are detected by the drones. This would reduce response times as there would be no need to wait for a civilian to report the fire. My main contribution was the development of one of the python codes of which it tallied up the number of incidents at each location and calculated the risk factor of a fire occurring at each location based on past incidents. This is to allow for better use of patrol drones at each area, regulating the number of drones based on the loactions' risk factor.
